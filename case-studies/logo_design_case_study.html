01 — Overview

Identity design that lasts.

A logo is the most concentrated expression of a brand — it has to communicate personality, professionalism, and purpose in a single mark, at any size, on any background. That constraint makes logo design one of the most distillation-heavy disciplines in visual design.

This collection brings together logo work for clients across different industries and brand contexts. Each mark was developed through a process of understanding the business, its audience, and the impression it needs to make — before a single vector path was drawn. The result is a set of logos that are not just visually considered, but strategically grounded.

All logos are delivered as scalable vector files (SVG, AI, EPS) alongside PNG exports for digital use — fully usable at any size from a business card to a billboard.

03 — Design Approach

What makes a logo actually work.

Logo design is as much about restraint as it is about creativity. The best logos are the ones that look inevitable — as if no other mark could have represented that brand.

Scalability
Every logo is tested at small sizes — favicon, app icon, embossed stamp — before finalisation. If it loses legibility at 32px, it needs rethinking. All marks are delivered as pure vectors.
Versatility
A logo must work on light and dark backgrounds, in colour and in single-colour (black or white). Each mark is tested across all variations before delivery so the client is never caught out.
Distinctiveness
The logo should be recognisable even without the company name attached. A strong mark has a clear visual idea — a shape, a tension, a letter form — that anchors it in memory.
Brand Alignment
Typography, weight, shape language, and colour all communicate personality before the name is read. A logo for a law firm should feel different from a logo for a children's brand — even at a glance.
