01 — Overview

What is Black Creek Industries?

Black Creek Industries Ltd is a construction and development company based in Campbell River, BC. They offer comprehensive turnkey services across five categories — marine infrastructure, civil works, residential renovations, commercial spaces, and snow removal — serving clients across Vancouver Island and beyond.

I work with the company as a Software Programmer at Black Creek Industries Ltd, and was brought in to redesign their existing Wix website, which had grown outdated as the business expanded its service offering and brand positioning. The old site no longer reflected the scale, quality, or full scope of what the company does.

The redesign rebuilt the site entirely from scratch within Wix — introducing a cleaner, more professional visual identity, restructuring the navigation to reflect all five service areas, and adding new sections including a 4-step process, BC Housing licensing credentials, Pacific Home Warranty approval, and a stronger homepage journey from introduction through to contact.

02 — The Old Site

What we were starting from.

The original website was built on Wix and had served the company in its earlier days. But as Black Creek Industries grew — adding marine and civil services, gaining BC Housing licensing, and taking on larger commercial projects — the old site could no longer keep up. It presented the company as a smaller residential contractor rather than the full-service construction firm it had become.

Before
Before
The original Wix website — before the redesign
⚠️
Incomplete service representation — The old site focused primarily on residential renovation, fencing, and snow removal. Marine infrastructure and civil works — major parts of the business — were either missing or buried.
⚠️
Weak brand presence — The visual design didn't convey the professionalism of a licensed, warranted construction company. The hero section was generic and the layout felt cluttered on both desktop and mobile.
⚠️
No credibility signals — BC Housing licensing and Pacific Home Warranty approval are significant trust markers. Neither was featured prominently on the old site.
⚠️
No clear process or journey — Visitors had no way to understand how working with the company actually worked. No process section, no structured path from landing to enquiry.

The new blackcreekindustriesltd.com

05 — Site Structure

Rebuilt navigation around five services.

The old navigation grouped everything under a generic "Services" page. The new structure gives each service category its own dedicated page — making it easier for the right client to find exactly what they need, and helping SEO by giving search engines distinct, focused pages for each service type.

06 — Homepage Sections

Every section earns its place.

The new homepage was structured as a deliberate narrative — each section answers the next question a prospective client would naturally have after reading the previous one.

Hero "Your Vision Built To Last" — bold headline, construction imagery, dual CTA: Contact Us + Learn More
Why Choose Us Comprehensive turnkey services, sustainability focus, zero-waste building practices — positions the company's values clearly
Trust Badges BC Housing Licensed logo + Pacific Home Warranty badge — critical credibility signals missing from the old site
Services Grid 5-card service overview: Marine · Civil · Residential · Commercial · Snow Removal — each links to its dedicated page
Process 4-step visual flow: Planning → Design → Build → Deliver — gives clients confidence in how the company works
CTA Banner "Your dream project starts here" — full-width closing section driving Contact Us Today

08 — Responsive Design

Tested on every screen.

Construction clients browse on mobile as often as desktop — on site, checking project details, or doing quick research. All pages were built with responsive breakpoints in Elementor and tested across desktop, tablet, and mobile to ensure the navigation, service cards, imagery, and CTAs work correctly at every size.

11 — Reflections

What I learned.

01
A redesign is also a rebranding. The company hadn't changed — but how it presented itself online had fallen behind its actual capabilities. A redesign is as much about repositioning and confidence as it is about visual polish.
02
Credibility signals deserve prominent placement. BC Housing licensing and Pacific Home Warranty approval are meaningful differentiators in the construction market. Burying them — or leaving them out entirely — is a real missed opportunity. They now sit front and centre on the homepage.
03
Working within the existing platform has real advantages. Rebuilding within Wix meant zero migration risk, no content loss, and no retraining the client on a new CMS. Starting from scratch visually — while keeping the platform the same — delivered a completely transformed result with minimal disruption.
