01 — Overview

Designs that work hard in the real world.

Flyers and promotional posters live in competitive visual environments — shop windows, noticeboards, social media feeds, and street-level signage. They have a fraction of a second to catch attention, communicate the key message, and prompt action. That constraint makes them one of the most discipline-heavy areas of graphic design.

This collection brings together flyer and poster work across a range of event types and promotional contexts — grand openings, seasonal promotions, service announcements, and event marketing. Each piece was designed for a specific purpose, audience, and output format, whether destined for print production or digital distribution.

03 — Design Approach

What makes a flyer actually work.

Every flyer and poster in this collection was built around the same core principle — the most important information must be readable in under two seconds. Everything else exists to support that goal.

Visual Hierarchy
The headline is always the largest element. Supporting details (date, location, offer) follow in a clear reading order. Nothing competes with the primary message.
Typography
Bold, high-contrast type for the headline. Clean, readable secondary fonts for details. Type weight and size differences do the heavy lifting in guiding the eye.
Colour & Contrast
High contrast between background and text is non-negotiable for print. Colour palettes are kept tight — two or three colours maximum — to feel intentional rather than chaotic.
Print vs Digital
Print versions use CMYK colour profiles and include bleed. Digital versions are optimised for screen — lighter backgrounds, slightly different sizing — without rebuilding from scratch.

05 — Tools

The right tool for each job.

Different briefs call for different tools. Canva works well for fast turnaround and client-editable templates. Photoshop handles photo-composite work. Illustrator is the choice for vector-based designs that need to scale cleanly to A3 and beyond.
