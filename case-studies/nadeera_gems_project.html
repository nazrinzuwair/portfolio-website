01 — Overview

What is Nadeera Gems?

Nadeera Gems and Jewellers is a Sri Lanka-based gems and jewellery business selling certified natural precious and semi-precious stones, gem jewellery, and gold jewellery — both island-wide and worldwide. The business needed a proper online store to reach customers beyond their local market and accept orders internationally.

This was my first full e-commerce project, built in 2022. The brief was to create a complete online shop from scratch — product listings across multiple categories, a shopping cart, checkout, customer accounts, and order tracking. It was a significant technical scope for an early project, and completing it taught me a great deal about how WooCommerce works end-to-end.

The site is built on WordPress with Elementor for page layout and WooCommerce handling all the store functionality — from product catalogue management through to the order lifecycle.

02 — The Brief

Problem & Solution.

⚠️
The Problem
The business was selling gems and jewellery without a digital storefront — relying on in-person sales and informal channels. This limited their reach to the local market and made it impossible to serve international customers efficiently. They needed a proper online shop with product pages, a cart, checkout, and delivery tracking.
✅
The Solution
A complete WooCommerce store with three product category shops (Gem Stones, Gem Jewellery, Gold Jewellery), individual product pages with images and descriptions, a full cart-to-checkout flow, customer account registration and login, and an order tracking page — all on a visually elegant site suited to a jewellery brand.
03 — Project Scope

Building a complete e-commerce store is significantly more complex than a brochure site — it requires understanding WooCommerce's product, order, and user systems, configuring payment and shipping, and designing a purchase journey that converts browsers into buyers.

First impressions for a jewellery brand.

10 — Reflections

What I learned.

This was my first e-commerce project — completed in 2022, early in my freelance career. Looking back, there are things I would do differently today: stronger product photography guidance, a more refined visual system, and a cleaner mobile checkout experience. But completing this project was formative — it forced me to learn WooCommerce's full feature set end-to-end, and the foundations I built here directly informed all the WordPress work I have done since.

01
E-commerce is a system, not just a website. WooCommerce connects products, orders, customers, shipping, payments, and notifications into one flowing system. Understanding how all of these relate to each other — and how design decisions affect the commerce flow — was the most important thing I learned from this project.
02
Product photography is half the design. On a jewellery e-commerce site, the product images carry enormous weight. When images are inconsistent or low quality, no amount of layout work compensates. I learned to be much more proactive about photo requirements and client briefing on this front.
03
Every project teaches something the next one benefits from. This site, built early in my career, gave me the confidence and technical grounding to take on progressively more complex WordPress work — including the sites I've built since for Black Creek Industries, Black Creek Modular, and NR Creativo Solutions.
