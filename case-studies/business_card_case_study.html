01 — Overview

Small format, big first impression.

A business card is often the first physical piece of brand communication someone receives. In 85×55mm — roughly the size of a credit card — it has to communicate who you are, what you do, and how to reach you, while feeling consistent with the brand it represents. There's no room for anything superfluous.

This collection brings together business card work across different industries and brand personalities — from clean and minimal professional cards through to more expressive designs for creative businesses. Each design was developed with both the client's brand and the card's practical function in mind: it needs to be readable, memorable, and print-perfectly at small scale.

03 — Design Approach

What goes into a great business card.

The physical constraints of a business card force design discipline — every element must earn its place in 85×55mm.

Hierarchy in small space
Name and role are always the largest elements. Contact details follow at a clearly smaller scale. The logo anchors the design without competing with the name.
Front & Back
The front makes the impression — bold, on-brand, memorable. The back carries the practical details — phone, email, website, QR code — so the front stays clean.
Typography at scale
Body text on business cards prints at 7–9pt. Font choices that look fine at 12pt can become unreadable at 7pt. Every typeface is tested at final print size before delivery.
Brand consistency
Each card uses the client's brand colours, fonts, and logo treatment — the card feels like it belongs to the same family as their website, letterhead, and other materials.
